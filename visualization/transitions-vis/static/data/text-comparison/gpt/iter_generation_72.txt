\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:introduction}
The proliferation of Large Language Models (LLMs) has significantly impacted various sectors, from healthcare to finance, creating unprecedented opportunities and challenges. As these models become more sophisticated, ensuring their safe and ethical deployment has emerged as a critical concern~\cite{brundage2020toward}. Traditional AI safety measures, which primarily focus on technical solutions, may no longer suffice in addressing the complex socio-technical landscape in which LLMs operate~\cite{russell2015research}. For a detailed discussion on current challenges, refer to Section~\ref{sec:challenges}.

\section{Current Challenges in AI Safety}
\label{sec:challenges}
In recent years, several high-profile incidents have highlighted the vulnerabilities of AI systems. These include biases in decision-making processes~\cite{bolukbasi2016man}, privacy breaches~\cite{goodfellow2014privacy}, and the potential for misuse in generating disinformation~\cite{zellers2019neuralfakenews}. To tackle these issues, it is essential to adopt a holistic approach that incorporates insights from social sciences, such as psychology and sociology, to understand the broader implications of AI technologies~\cite{weng2021systematic}. Our proposed interdisciplinary approach, discussed in Section~\ref{sec:approach}, addresses these challenges.

\section{Interdisciplinary Approach}
\label{sec:approach}
Our research advocates for a paradigm shift in AI safety, urging the integration of interdisciplinary approaches that incorporate insights from social sciences to better understand and mitigate risks associated with LLMs. As LLMs continue to evolve, the need for comprehensive and adaptive security measures becomes increasingly critical~\cite{brundage2020toward}. By combining technical expertise with social science methodologies, we can develop more robust frameworks for AI governance~\cite{floridi2018ai}. The effectiveness of this approach is illustrated through case studies in Section~\ref{sec:case_studies}.

\section{Case Studies}

To illustrate the effectiveness of this interdisciplinary approach, we present several case studies where social science frameworks have been successfully integrated into AI safety protocols. The implications of these findings are further discussed in the conclusion in Section~\ref{sec:conclusion}.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
In conclusion, addressing the risks associated with LLMs requires a comprehensive approach that goes beyond traditional technical solutions. By integrating insights from social sciences, we can better anticipate and mitigate potential harms, ensuring that AI technologies are deployed in a safe and ethical manner. Future research should continue to explore interdisciplinary collaborations as a means to enhance AI safety~\cite{bostrom2014superintelligence}.
